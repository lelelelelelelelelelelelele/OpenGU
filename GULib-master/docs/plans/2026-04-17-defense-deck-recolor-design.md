# Defense Deck Recolor Design

**Context**

The current defense deck works structurally, but the maroon-led theme makes the slides feel heavier and more templated than intended. The user wants the presentation to feel more academic and mature, with less "AI deck" flavor, while keeping a formal defense tone.

**Approved Direction**

- Primary visual language: deep blue, not maroon
- Accent language: red only for risk, fragility, caution, and attack-related emphasis
- Remove the gold accent that contributes to a dated template feel
- Keep dark full-slide cover and closing slides for formal presentation tone
- Do not adopt an official NUS template; instead, aim for a deck that feels institutionally appropriate without looking standardized

**Design Goals**

1. Make the deck feel more like a technical academic defense
2. Reduce the sense of repeated "theme color everywhere"
3. Preserve contrast and hierarchy on title, summary, and result slides
4. Reduce AI-like visual sameness by using more white cards and fewer colored blocks

**Palette Strategy**

- Deep navy for headers, dark slides, and primary callout cards
- Soft off-white / warm paper tone for content-slide backgrounds
- Neutral gray border for white cards
- Red only for vulnerability or caution emphasis
- Replace the previous gold role with either white cards or dark neutral emphasis

**Execution Scope**

- Update global color constants in the PPT generator
- Rebalance card fills so most informational cards return to white
- Keep only a small number of red emphasis elements on relevant slides
- Re-render the deck and visually inspect key slides

**Non-Goals**

- No slide-order changes
- No content rewrite beyond what is required by the palette rebalance
- No adoption of a separate institutional template system
