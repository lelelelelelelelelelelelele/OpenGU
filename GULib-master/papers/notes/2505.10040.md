# 2505.10040 - Instance-Prototype Affinity Learning for Non-Exemplar Continual Graph Learning

- one-sentence claim: Graph Neural Networks (GNN) endure catastrophic forgetting, undermining their capacity to preserve previously acquired knowledge amid the assimilation of novel information.
- task setting + threat model: Task: graph unlearning (remove influence of nodes/edges or samples in GNN-based learning). Threat model: deletion requests; potential residual memorization/privacy leakage after unlearning.
- method sketch:
  - Rehearsal-based techniques revisit historical examples, adopted as a principal strategy to alleviate this phenomenon.
  - However, memory explosion and privacy infringements impose significant constraints on their utility.
  - Non-Exemplar methods circumvent the prior issues through Prototype Replay (PR), yet feature drift presents new challenges.
  - Uses graph-specific unlearning operators or update rules instead of full retraining.
  - Transfers knowledge from a larger/teacher graph model into a compact student model.
  - Evaluates privacy risk with attack-based auditing signals.

  - Integrate its privacy auditing attack protocol into GU evaluation as a leakage metric.
  - Replicate its ablation protocol to test sensitivity to deletion ratio and graph sparsity.
