# 2408.09705 - Community-Centric Graph Unlearning

- one-sentence claim: Graph unlearning technology has become increasingly important since the advent of the `right to be forgotten' and the growing concerns about the privacy and security of artificial intelligence.
- task setting + threat model: Task: graph unlearning (remove influence of nodes/edges or samples in GNN-based learning). Threat model: deletion requests; potential residual memorization/privacy leakage after unlearning.
- method sketch:
  - Graph unlearning aims to quickly eliminate the effects of specific data on graph neural networks (GNNs).
  - However, most existing deterministic graph unlearning frameworks follow a balanced partition-submodel training-aggregation paradigm, resulting in a lack of structural information between subgraph neighborhoods and redundant unlearning parameter calculations.
  - To address this issue, we propose a novel Graph Structure Mapping Unlearning paradigm (GSMU) and a novel method based on it named Community-centric Graph Eraser (CGE).
  - Uses graph-specific unlearning operators or update rules instead of full retraining.
  - Partitions graph structure or training workload to reduce memory/communication costs.
  - Evaluates privacy risk with attack-based auditing signals.
- evaluation: datasets/baselines/metrics: datasets=ppi; baselines=gat; metrics=ap, efficiency
- possible reuse for GU:
  - Use this unlearning mechanism as a baseline method in OpenGU benchmark comparisons.
  - Integrate its privacy auditing attack protocol into GU evaluation as a leakage metric.
  - Replicate its ablation protocol to test sensitivity to deletion ratio and graph sparsity.
