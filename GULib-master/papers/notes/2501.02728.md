# 2501.02728 - OpenGU: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Graph Unlearning

- one-sentence claim: Graph Machine Learning is essential for understanding and analyzing relational data.
- task setting + threat model: Task: graph unlearning (remove influence of nodes/edges or samples in GNN-based learning). Threat model: deletion requests; potential residual memorization/privacy leakage after unlearning.
- method sketch:
  - However, privacy-sensitive applications demand the ability to efficiently remove sensitive information from trained graph neural networks (GNNs), avoiding the unnecessary time and space overhead caused by retraining models from scratch.
  - To address this issue, Graph Unlearning (GU) has emerged as a critical solution, with the potential to support dynamic graph updates in data management systems and enable scalable unlearning in distributed data systems while ensuring privacy compliance.
  - Unlike machine unlearning in computer vision or other fields, GU faces unique difficulties due to the non-Euclidean nature of graph data and the recursive message-passing mechanism of GNNs.
  - Uses graph-specific unlearning operators or update rules instead of full retraining.
  - Evaluates privacy risk with attack-based auditing signals.
