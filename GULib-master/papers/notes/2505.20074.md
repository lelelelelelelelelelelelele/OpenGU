# 2505.20074 - An Out-Of-Distribution Membership Inference Attack Approach for Cross-Domain Graph Attacks

- one-sentence claim: Graph Neural Network-based methods face privacy leakage risks due to the introduction of topological structures about the targets, which allows attackers to bypass the target's prior knowledge of the sensitive attributes and realize membership inference attacks (MIA) by observing and analyzing the topology distribution.
- task setting + threat model: Task: privacy auditing / membership inference / privacy-preserving graph learning. Threat model: adversary infers membership or sensitive attributes from model outputs/embeddings.
- method sketch:
  - As privacy concerns grow, the assumption of MIA, which presumes that attackers can obtain an auxiliary dataset with the same distribution, is increasingly deviating from reality.
  - In this paper, we categorize the distribution diversity issue in real-world MIA scenarios as an Out-Of-Distribution (OOD) problem, and propose a novel Graph OOD Membership Inference Attack (GOOD-MIA) to achieve cross-domain graph attacks.
  - Specifically, we construct shadow subgraphs with distributions from different domains to model the diversity of real-world data.
  - Uses influence-style approximations to estimate parameter/data impact under graph edits.
  - Evaluates privacy risk with attack-based auditing signals.
