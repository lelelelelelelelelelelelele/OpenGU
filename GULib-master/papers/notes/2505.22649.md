# 2505.22649 - Pre-training for Recommendation Unlearning

- one-sentence claim: Modern recommender systems powered by Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) excel at modeling complex user-item interactions, yet increasingly face scenarios requiring selective forgetting of training data.
- task setting + threat model: Task: graph unlearning (remove influence of nodes/edges or samples in GNN-based learning). Threat model: deletion requests; potential residual memorization/privacy leakage after unlearning.
- method sketch:
  - Beyond user requests to remove specific interactions due to privacy concerns or preference changes, regulatory frameworks mandate recommender systems' ability to eliminate the influence of certain user data from models.
  - This recommendation unlearning challenge presents unique difficulties as removing connections within interaction graphs creates ripple effects throughout the model, potentially impacting recommendations for numerous users.
  - Traditional approaches suffer from significant drawbacks: fragmentation methods damage graph structure and diminish performance, while influence function techniques make assumptions that may not hold in complex GNNs, particularly with self-supervised or random architectures.
  - Uses graph-specific unlearning operators or update rules instead of full retraining.
  - Uses influence-style approximations to estimate parameter/data impact under graph edits.
  - Evaluates privacy risk with attack-based auditing signals.

  - Integrate its privacy auditing attack protocol into GU evaluation as a leakage metric.
  - Reuse influence estimation to prioritize which nodes/edges to retrain after deletion requests.
