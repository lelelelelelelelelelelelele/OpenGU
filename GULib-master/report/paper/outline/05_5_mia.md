## Claim

Deletion-visibility axis (update-detection AUC) and utility axis (F1 drop) are **not aligned**. Some methods that resist F1 attack still expose the deleted set through posterior shift; some that bleed F1 do not. This makes the audit a complementary diagnostic, not a calibrated cross-family privacy ranking.

## Content

- Cross-method update-detection AUC table — post Phase A bug-fix (sanity values: MEGU 0.37, GraphEraser 0.60 — see dashboard §3.1)
- Note: pre-2026-05-04 JSONs have legacy field `mia_auc=0.000` for MEGU/IDEA/shard-family due to bug — Phase B regenerates clean data
- Protocol: positives are requested-for-unlearning nodes, negatives are held-out test nodes, score is before-vs-after posterior $L_2$ shift, metric is ROC-AUC
- Decoupling discussion: e.g., MEGU may show lower deletion visibility even with near-null F1 effect, while shard methods may show higher update visibility without utility collapse
- Related/future-work baseline: Olatunji et al. shadow-model MIA; do not describe the reported Phase B metric as standard shadow-model MIA
