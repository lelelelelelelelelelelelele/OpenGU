\begin{abstract}
Machine unlearning---removing specific data from trained models without full
retraining---is increasingly mandated by privacy regulation and routinely
deployed in graph learning systems where deletion requests target individual
nodes or edges. \emph{Approximate} graph unlearning (GU) methods trade exact
removal for orders-of-magnitude speedup, but the resulting approximation gap
is, we show, \emph{adversarially exploitable} by an attacker who controls
the deletion set.

We present, to our knowledge, the first systematic adversarial audit of
approximate GU. The attacker chooses a small deletion set---using
influence maximization, pseudo-influence functions, or their hybrid---to
amplify approximation error. We evaluate \textbf{six} methods covering
three OpenGU categories with intra-family pairs: Partition-based
(GraphEraser, GraphRevoker), Influence-Function-based (GIF, IDEA), and
Learning-based (GNNDelete, MEGU), across Cora, Citeseer, and ogbn-arxiv
(169K nodes) with GCN/GAT backbones. This audit reveals extreme 
heterogeneity in vulnerability profiles across methods.

Our \textbf{Vulnerability Fingerprint} projects each method onto two
attack-specific F1 axes: structural spread (IM) and gradient signal
(TracIn). It separates three regimes. \textbf{GNNDelete} suffers the
largest collapse (\interim{16\%} average F1, peaks above
\interim{27\%}); \textbf{GIF} shows a smaller but significant
TracIn-aligned effect (paired effect $\approx$\interim{+4\%} F1,
$p$\interim{$<$0.001}); and the Partition pair shows no attack-specific
amplification, instead exhibiting a \textbf{Shard Protection Effect} in
which deletion improves F1 by \interim{3--6\%}. MEGU and IDEA remain near
the origin ($<$\interim{1\%} F1), exposing strong within-family
heterogeneity.

We further introduce a compact diagnostics suite---$\Delta
F_{\text{total}}=\Delta F_{\text{arch}}+\Delta F_{\text{attack}}$,
retrain gap, prediction shift, and hop-distance decay---for separating
data-loss effects from approximation drift in future GU audits.
\end{abstract}
